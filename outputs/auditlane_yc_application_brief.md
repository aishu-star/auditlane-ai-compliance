# Auditlane YC Application Brief

Date: July 2, 2026

## Company

Auditlane is an AI-native compliance operations service. It does not sell another compliance dashboard. It sells completed work: security questionnaires, audit evidence packets, control refreshes, and exception memos for B2B SaaS, fintech, and healthcare companies.

## One-Liner

Auditlane replaces manual compliance evidence work with an AI-operated service desk that ships QA-approved audit packets in 48 hours.

## YC RFS Fit

This directly targets YC's Rank 1 opportunity from the analysis: AI-Native Service Companies.

The company sells a service outcome, not software seats. Compliance, audit prep, and security questionnaires already have budget, urgency, repeated workflows, and expensive human labor. AI can collapse delivery time while human QA keeps enterprise trust.

## Problem

Growing B2B companies lose weeks to repetitive compliance work:

- Enterprise security questionnaires block deals.
- SOC 2, HIPAA, ISO, and GDPR evidence collection pulls engineers and security leaders away from core work.
- Answers are scattered across policies, tickets, cloud exports, GitHub logs, docs, and old questionnaires.
- Existing tools help store evidence, but customers still need humans to do the work.

## Customer

Initial buyer:

- Seed to Series C B2B SaaS companies.
- 20-500 employees.
- Selling to enterprise customers.
- Small security or compliance team.
- High volume of questionnaires, audit evidence requests, and customer security reviews.

Economic buyer:

- VP Security.
- Head of Compliance.
- COO.
- Head of Sales for companies where security reviews block revenue.

## MVP

The first product is an AI-native service cockpit:

- Intake requests, policies, prior answers, tickets, cloud exports, and docs.
- Map requests to controls and frameworks.
- Retrieve evidence from source systems.
- Draft customer-ready answers with citations.
- Route exceptions to human QA.
- Ship finished packets and memos.
- Track margin, turnaround time, source confidence, and customer savings.

## Why Now

AI models can now read documents, reason over workflows, draft structured responses, and operate across fragmented tools. At the same time, enterprise sales teams face more security reviews, compliance frameworks are multiplying, and companies want outcomes rather than another tool to manage.

## Pricing

Start outcome-priced:

- $500-$1,500 per security questionnaire.
- $2,500-$8,000 per audit evidence packet.
- $5,000-$20,000 monthly managed compliance operations plan.

Later expand into platform fees for customers with high volume.

## Proof YC Will Care About

Best early metrics:

- 5 paid pilots in 14-21 days.
- 48-hour median turnaround.
- 70%+ of manual work automated.
- 90%+ source-backed answer accuracy.
- 60%+ gross margin after human QA.
- Evidence that customers repeat after the first packet.

## 14-Day Validation Plan

Day 1-2:

- Interview 20 security, compliance, and sales leaders.
- Collect real examples of questionnaires and audit evidence requests.

Day 3-5:

- Run concierge fulfillment manually with AI assistance.
- Deliver 2 finished packets to friendly companies.

Day 6-10:

- Productize the cockpit around intake, evidence retrieval, QA, and delivery.
- Charge first customers per packet.

Day 11-14:

- Measure turnaround time, hours saved, accuracy, and willingness to pay.
- Convert pilots into monthly managed service plans.

## YC Application Answer Drafts

What are you making?

We are building Auditlane, an AI-native compliance operations service that replaces manual audit prep and security questionnaire work. Customers send us requests, and we deliver completed, citation-backed evidence packets in 48 hours with human QA.

Who needs it?

B2B SaaS, fintech, and healthcare companies selling to enterprises. These teams repeatedly lose engineering, security, and sales time to compliance requests that are urgent but repetitive.

Why will it be big?

Compliance services are a large labor market, and every enterprise software sale creates more security and audit work. We start by replacing one painful service, then expand into the operating layer for compliance, governance, risk, and revenue enablement.

What is new?

Instead of selling software that helps a customer do compliance work, we sell the completed work. AI agents handle control mapping, evidence retrieval, answer drafting, and citation assembly; humans review exceptions and quality.
