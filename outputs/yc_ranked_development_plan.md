# YC RFS Ranked Development Plan

Date: July 2, 2026

This plan converts the YC RFS analysis into a build order. The ranking is based on YC-style criteria: urgent problem, clear customer, fast MVP, strong "why now", large market, founder-speed advantage, and evidence that can be shown before applying.

## Ranking Logic

YC usually responds well to startups that show:

- A painful, specific problem.
- A narrow first customer.
- A product that can be built and tested quickly.
- Early usage, pilots, revenue, or strong expert validation.
- A market that can become huge after a focused wedge.
- Founder insight that feels earned, not generic.

## Recommended Development Order

### Phase 1: Pick the Best Wedge

Start with one of these three unless you have rare domain access:

1. AI-Native Service Company
2. Software for Agents
3. Company Brain

These are strongest because they are capital-efficient, buildable by a small team, easy to validate with customers, and very aligned with YC's current AI-native thesis.

### Phase 2: Define the First Customer

Do not start with "companies" or "enterprises" broadly. Start with a very specific buyer.

Good examples:

- AI-native compliance service for fintech startups.
- Agent-native CRM update tool for sales teams using AI agents.
- Company Brain for customer support teams with messy policy docs.
- PM tool for founder-led B2B SaaS teams with many customer calls.
- Physical-work guidance for HVAC technician training.

### Phase 3: Build the MVP in 7-14 Days

The MVP should not be a full platform. It should prove one painful workflow.

For AI-Native Services:

- Choose one deliverable customers already pay for.
- Use AI internally to produce it faster.
- Keep humans in the loop for quality.
- Charge for the finished result.

For Software for Agents:

- Build one API/MCP/CLI that lets agents complete a real workflow.
- Add excellent docs and predictable auth.
- Measure completion rate and time saved.

For Company Brain:

- Ingest one company's messy docs, Slack exports, tickets, or policies.
- Convert them into structured SOPs.
- Let an agent use those SOPs to complete a bounded task.

## YC Application Angle by Top Idea

### 1. AI-Native Service Company

YC pitch:

"We replace [expensive outsourced service] for [specific customer] using an AI-native operating system. Customers currently spend [$X] and wait [Y days]. We deliver the same output in [Z time] at [lower cost/better quality]."

Best metrics:

- Revenue.
- Gross margin improvement.
- Turnaround time reduction.
- Customer repeat usage.

### 2. Software for Agents

YC pitch:

"Agents are forced to use human software through brittle UIs. We make [workflow] agent-native through APIs/MCP/CLI, so agents can complete [task] reliably without human intervention."

Best metrics:

- Agent task success rate.
- Time saved per task.
- Number of agent calls.
- Number of developers/teams integrating.

### 3. Company Brain

YC pitch:

"AI agents fail inside companies because company knowledge is scattered. We turn messy operational knowledge into executable workflows that agents and employees can safely use."

Best metrics:

- Knowledge retrieval accuracy.
- SOP freshness.
- Reduction in repeated questions.
- Number of tasks agents complete using the brain.

### 4. Cursor for Product Managers

YC pitch:

"Coding agents make building cheaper, but teams still do not know what to build. We turn customer evidence into product specs, UI changes, data model changes, and coding-agent-ready tasks."

Best metrics:

- Number of customer conversations processed.
- Specs generated and shipped.
- Reduction in product discovery time.
- Adoption by founders/PMs.

### 5. AI Guidance for Physical Work

YC pitch:

"Skilled labor is scarce. We give workers real-time AI guidance through phones or smart glasses so they can perform complex tasks with less training."

Best metrics:

- Training time reduction.
- Task completion rate.
- Error/rework reduction.
- Paid pilots with field-service companies.

## Ideas to Delay Unless You Have Strong Founder-Market Fit

These are valuable but should not be first unless you have rare expertise, capital, or access:

- AI Personalized Medicine.
- Inference Chips for Agent Workflows.
- Large Spatial Models.
- Electronics in Space.
- Industrial Capabilities in Space.
- Modern Metal Mills.
- Counter-Swarm Defense.
- AI-Native Hedge Funds.

They are not bad ideas. They are just much harder to validate quickly in a YC-style timeline.

## My Suggested First Build

The best first build is:

## Company Brain for Support or Sales Ops

Why:

- It fits YC's AI automation thesis.
- It is buildable quickly.
- Every company has messy internal knowledge.
- It can expand into a broader AI operating system.
- The MVP can be tested with startups before selling to larger companies.

First version:

1. Upload docs, FAQs, Slack exports, support tickets, and policy notes.
2. Extract workflows, decisions, exceptions, and source citations.
3. Generate clean SOPs.
4. Let an AI agent answer or execute one support/sales workflow.
5. Track confidence, source citations, and human approvals.

First target customers:

- B2B SaaS startups with 5-50 support or sales people.
- Companies with growing customer volume and messy internal docs.
- Founder-led companies where knowledge is trapped in Slack and people's heads.

First proof:

- "We reduced support onboarding time from 10 days to 3 days."
- "We cut repeated Slack questions by 40%."
- "Our agent resolved 30% of policy-based support tickets with human approval."
- "A new hire could follow generated SOPs without asking a senior teammate."
